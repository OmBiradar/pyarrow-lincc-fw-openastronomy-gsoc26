Some conclusions from the above colab notebook:

- loading from RAM is faster than SSD while reading parquet files in 98.88% of
  the cases. It's about 10% to 20% faster than SSD on various sizes of files.
- **Optimization Success:** The parallelized struct reader (OPTIMIZED branch)
  improves multi-threaded nested read performance by an average of **32.15%**
  (peaking at **55.21%**) on large datasets (Orders 6-8) compared to the MAIN branch.
- **Threading Threshold Validation:** Multi-threading introduces overhead for
  micro-files (Orders 1-3), causing a **5.22%** regression on average compared
  to single-threaded reads. However, multi-threading crosses the break-even point
  and becomes strictly beneficial at **Order 3**.
- **Compression Algorithm Scaling:** The optimization yields the highest gains
  on CPU-intensive compression algorithms like ZSTD and BROTLI (averaging
  **42.79%** faster than MAIN). Conversely, extremely lightweight algorithms
  like SNAPPY hit memory bandwidth limits sooner, showing smaller overall
  multi-threading gains (**19.41%**).
